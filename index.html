Famous Ravis Throughout History

Ravi Shankar (1920-2012)

One of the most famous Indian musicians of all time, Ravi Shankar was a legendary sitar player who influenced countless musicians worldwide. He was known for introducing Indian classical music to the Western world and collaborated with artists like George Harrison of The Beatles.

Notable achievements: Multiple Grammy Awards, Bharat Ratna (India's highest civilian honor), Kennedy Center Honors

Sir C.V. Raman (1888-1970)

Born as Chandrasekhara Venkata Raman, he was an Indian physicist who won the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1930 for his groundbreaking work on light scattering. The Raman effect, named after him, demonstrates that when light traverses a transparent material, some of the deflected light changes its wavelength.

Notable achievements: Nobel Prize in Physics (1930), Bharat Ratna, Lenin Peace Prize

Ravi Varma (1848-1906)

Raja Ravi Varma was a celebrated Indian painter and artist who is considered one of the greatest painters in the history of Indian art. He was known for combining European artistic techniques with Indian imagery and is particularly remembered for his paintings depicting scenes from the epics Mahabharata and Ramayana.

Notable achievements: Kaiser-i-Hind Gold Medal, Established the first lithographic press in India

Ravi Zacharias (1946-2020)

An Indian-born Canadian-American Christian apologist, Ravi Zacharias was a renowned author and speaker who founded Ravi Zacharias International Ministries (RZIM). He wrote numerous books on Christian apologetics and spoke at venues worldwide about faith, philosophy, and religious beliefs.

Notable achievements: Author of over 30 books, Founder of RZIM, Distinguished global speaker

Ravi Shastri (b. 1962)

A former Indian cricketer and commentator, Ravi Shastri served as the head coach of the Indian national cricket team. As a player, he was a member of the Indian team that won the 1983 Cricket World Cup. He was also named Champion of Champions in the World Championship of Cricket in 1985.

Notable achievements: 1983 Cricket World Cup winner, Champion of Champions (1985), Successful tenure as Team India's head coach
